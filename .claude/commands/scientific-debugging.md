# Scientific Debugging

## Problem

As an AI assistant, you often approach debugging by trying random fixes based on pattern matching rather than systematically investigating the root cause of issues, which can lead to inefficient troubleshooting and incomplete understanding.

## Example Scenarios

- You focus on fixing symptoms rather than addressing root causes
- You apply solutions without verifying assumptions
- You suggest multiple unrelated changes simultaneously
- You get caught in debugging loops trying random fixes
- You focus too much on error messages without investigating underlying mechanics
- You struggle with environment configuration issues

## Recommended Actions

1. **Formulate hypotheses**: Before attempting fixes, form clear hypotheses about what might be causing the issue and how to verify them.

2. **Test one change at a time**: Suggest incremental, isolated changes to test each hypothesis rather than applying multiple fixes simultaneously.

3. **Gather diagnostic information**: Request logs, debugging output, and results from controlled experiments to collect evidence before proposing solutions.

4. **State your assumptions**: Clearly articulate your assumptions about how the system works and how you plan to verify them.

5. **Leverage user domain knowledge**: Ask specific questions to gain insights from the user, especially for complex debugging scenarios that require deeper understanding.

## Anti-patterns to Avoid

- Randomly trying fixes based on superficial pattern matching
- Suggesting solutions without explaining why they should work
- Getting stuck in debugging loops where you try increasingly desperate fixes
- Proposing a fix without explaining the underlying cause
- Attempting to debug environment configuration issues through trial and error

## Remember

Scientific debugging may take more time initially but builds deeper understanding and leads to more robust solutions. Always approach debugging systematically by forming and testing hypotheses rather than jumping to random fixes based on pattern matching.
